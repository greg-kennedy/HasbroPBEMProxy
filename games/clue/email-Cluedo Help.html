Solving the Mystery:
The Goal of em@il-Cluedo		TOP é

Dr. Black has been found murdered in one of the rooms of his mansion, apparently the victim of foul play. To win the game, you must determine the correct answers to the following three questions:

  1. Who done it?
  2. Where?
  3. With what weapon?

Who could be 1 of 6 suspects represented by coloured tokens on the game board: Colonel Mustard, Miss Scarlett, Professor Plum, Rev. Green, Mrs. White, and Mrs. Peacock.

Where could be in 1 of 9 rooms: Hall, Study, Library, Billiard Room, Conservatory, Kitchen, Dining Room, Lounge, or the Ballroom.

With could be with 1 of 6 weapons: Rope, Lead Pipe, Dagger, Spanner, Candlestick, or the Revolver.

A deck of cards containing images of all the Suspects, Rooms, and Weapons will be dealt to the players at the beginning of the game. But not before one card from each category (one Suspect, one Weapon, and one Room) is removed and hidden in the Confidential Case file. The cards will be revealed at the end of the game, when a correct accusation is made.

Email Games OverviewTOP é

Hasbro Interactive em@il games are played by sending turns back-and-forth via email to your opponents. To view your opponent's last move, simply open the attached "TURN" file they emailed to you. This will automatically open up the proper email game and let you play the next move.

It's that easy!


Starting a New GameTOP é

NOTE: Only owners of the retail version can initiate an em@il game.

  1. To start a game, select em@il-Cluedo from the Start menu and launch the client.
  2. Click the Begin Game button to start a new game.
  3. Enter your name and email address into the corresponding fields and click OK.
  4. Enter the next player's email address and click OK. You will need a minimum of three people, including you, to play em@il-Cluedo.

    NOTE: Any email address you enter will be stored in the Address field which you can access by clicking the drop-down arrow next to the entry field. Here you can easily store email addresses. To delete and add email addresses, click the Address Book button on the start screen.

  5. After three players have been specified, you will have the option to add another player (up to six). Click Yes to add another player; No to start the game.

    NOTE: After the sixth player's email address is entered, the game will automatically begin.

  6. Choose a card option by clicking either Automatic or Manual disprove. Click Automatic if you want the game to reveal the cards automatically. Click Manual if you want everyone in the game to reveal a card of their choice.

    NOTE: Gameplay will be slightly different depending on the option you choose. In the Manual style of play, players will choose any suggested cards they'd like to reveal and see cards that have been revealed to them. In Automatic, players will instantly see a disproving card from the next player able to show one. However, the revealed card will be the game's choice, not the person's.

  7. Choose a die rolling option. Click Classic to play em@il-Cluedo with the six-sided die. Click Speed to play a version of Cluedo that replaces the die with nine moves that can be used every turn. Players use these nine moves by clicking on board squares (one move per square) or using secret passages, making suggestions, and accusations (three apiece).
  8. Every player in the game represents one of the suspects. Click on the suspect you'd like to represent in the game.
  9. Now wait as the deck of cards are shuffled and the secret cards are stored away in the Confidential Case file. For the duration of the game, you will try to determine which three cards hold the solution to who killed Dr. Black...
  10. When you first begin the game, your hand of cards will display as your notepad automatically fills in. These cards tell you something important: They are not in the Confidential Case file, and therefore cannot be the solution to the game. Click OK to access the game board and start playing.


Joining a GameTOP é

Someone you know has invited you to join a game of em@il-Cluedo. You can find out from the Join Game window what style of game you are playing (the disprove and die-roll options) as well as see how many other players have been invited.

  1. Enter your name into the field and click OK.
  2. Every player in the game represents one of the suspects. Click on the suspect you'd like to represent in the game.
  3. When you first begin the game, your hand of cards will display as your notepad automatically fills in. These cards tell you something important: They are not in the Confidential Case file, and therefore cannot be the solution to the game. Click OK to access the game board and start playing.


Navigating the Game BoardTOP é

The em@il-Cluedo game window contains buttons with pop-up text as well as labelled buttons. Everything you can click on is described in detail below:

Help
Click this button to read the help, which includes the complete game rules.

Read Messages
Click this button to read any messages sent by your opponents.

Write Message
Click this button to write a message that will be sent to everyone in the game.

Resign
Click this button if you wish to resign from the current game.

Minimise
Click this button to minimise the game to the taskbar.

Maximise
Click this button to maximise the game so that it may be played in full-screen mode.

Close Game
Click this button to close the game window.

View Cards
Click this button to view the hand of cards you have been dealt in the game.

History
Click this button to access the History window. From here, you can play back everything that has happened since your last turn.

Notepad
Click in the first column of the notepad to fill in the colours which correspond to your cards and to those shown to you by other players. Click in the second column to cycle through an X and a question mark ?. Feel free to use these symbols to help keep track of your suggestions.

Die
The die toggles back and forth depending on whether you're playing Classic Cluedo or Speed Cluedo. The Classic game flashes a question mark symbol until you click on it to roll the die. Speed Cluedo has nine moves you can use. The die will "count down" as you move around the board or make suggestions.

Suggest
Click this button to make a suggestion. You'll choose a suspect and a weapon (the room is your current location on the game board). If another player can disprove your suggestion, you will be shown one of their cards.

Accuse
Click this button to make an accusation, whereby you'll choose a room, suspect, and weapon. Players can only make one accusation per game.

Done
Click this button if you want to stop your token mid-move or when you cannot do anything more in the game.

Exit Cluedo
Click this button to exit em@il-Cluedo.

Game Board Squares
The "tiles" on the game board are the movement squares. Click on them to travel around the game board.

Secret Passages
The white squares in the Study, Lounge, Conservatory, and Kitchen are secret passageways. Players located in one of these rooms may click on the secret passage to travel diagonally across the game board to the opposite room.


Rolling the DieTOP é

  • Classic Cluedo If you are playing a game using the Classic die, click on the blinking die in the centre of the game board. Whatever you roll is the amount of squares you can move.

  • Speed Cluedo If you are playing Speed Cluedo, you will have a default of nine moves to use (and the die in the centre of the game board will display the number "9"). You do not need to roll the die. You can use these nine moves by clicking on board squares (one move per square) or using Secret Passages, making suggestions, and accusations (three moves apiece).


Moving Your TokenTOP é

To move on the game board, click the squares, one at a time, to mark out the path your token should follow. You will notice that each valid square will be marked with a number indicating the move number you placed.

After you click on the final square, your token will automatically move to that spot. Or, if you don't want to use all the die moves, you can simply click the Done button.

Notes

  • You may move horizontally or vertically, forward or backward, but not diagonally.
  • You may change directions as many times as your roll allows. However, you may not enter the same square twice on the same turn.
  • You may not pass through or land on a square that's occupied by another token.
  • If you change your mind about where to move your token, you may re-click any of your previous squares, and your move will progress from that point.
  • If playing Speed Cluedo, simply move your token up to nine squares or until you get into a room. You may also move to a particular game board square and press Done -- without expending all your moves.
  • The rooms in opposite corners of the mansion (the Study, Lounge, Kitchen, and Conservatory) are connected by secret passages. If you're in one of these rooms at the start of your turn, you may use a secret passage instead of rolling. To move through a secret passage, click on the white passage square; your token will automatically reappear in the opposite corner room.


Entering and Leaving RoomsTOP é

  • When you pass through a door, the door is not counted as a space.
  • You may not pass through a door that's blocked by an opponent's token.
  • You do not need an exact roll to enter a room.
  • You may not re-enter a room on a single turn.
  • If your opponents block all doors and trap you in a room, you must wait for someone to move and unblock a door so you can leave.


Making SuggestionsTOP é

During the game, players take turns deducing the identity of the three cards hidden in the Confidential Case file by making suggestions. A suggestion includes, Who did it? Where? And With what weapon? For example, someone might suggest that, "Miss Scarlett killed Dr. Black in the Lounge with the Revolver."

As soon as you enter a room, you may make a suggestion by clicking the Suggest button. Then click on a Suspect, then a Weapon, and press OK to confirm your suggestion.

Notes

  • Remember: When making a suggestion, be sure to consider all tokens -- including spare ones -- as falling under equal suspicion.
  • When playing a game of Speed Cluedo, you need at least three moves left on your die to make a suggestion.
  • When making a suggestion, you may name your cards in order to gain information or to mislead your opponents.
  • You may, if you wish, make an accusation after making a suggestion (unless you're playing Speed Cluedo and have less than three moves left).
  • You may make only one suggestion per turn. You may not forfeit a turn to remain in a particular room. But if you're trapped in a room because your opponents are blocking the doors, you must remain there (unable to make suggestions) until a door is unblocked and you can move out of the room.
  • If your token was moved to a room as a result of a suggestion, you may, on your next run, do one of two things: move from the room in one of the usual ways OR make a suggestion from that room.


Disproving SuggestionsTOP é

As soon as you make a suggestion, the game checks out the playing order and automatically determines which of your opponents can disprove your suggestion. If your game is using the Manual Disprove option, an opponent with a disproving card will have a chance to choose which one to reveal. If you are playing an Auto Disprove game, you will see the revealed card right away and the player who "revealed" it will be updated through their History window.

If no one is able to prove your suggestion false, you may either click the End Turn button to send your turn to the next player or Accuse to make an accusation.


Making AccusationsTOP é

When you think you know which three cards are in the Confidential Case file, you may make an accusation on your turn. Click the Accuse button and select a Room, a Suspect, and a Weapon. Click OK to confirm the accusation.

Notes

  • You may make only one accusation per game.
  • If incorrect, you may not make any more moves in the game.


Winning the GameTOP é

A player wins when they make a successful accusation -- by determining the Suspect, Weapon, and Room cards hidden in the Confidential Case file. When this happens, all players are emailed and shown the three solution cards as well as who won.


Reviewing Round HistoryTOP é

Click the History button to view all the moves that have happened since your last turn. The players are listed in order at the top of the window. Click a player's name or the Next button to see what they did; click the Playback button to view their token movement on the game board as well as any suggestions or accusations they have made. Click OK to exit the History window.


Reading and Writing MessagesTOP é

To send text messages to all the other players in the game, click the Write Message button. Type a message in the text entry field and then click OK.

If someone has written a message, the Read Message icon above the Notepad will flash. To read the message, click the Read Message button; to close the window, click OK.

NOTE: Hasbro Interactive does not monitor, control, endorse, or accept responsibility for the content of text messages transmitted through the use of this product. Use of the optional Text Message feature is at your own risk. Users are strongly encouraged not to give out personal information through text message transmissions.


Sending Your TurnTOP é

When you have moved all you can or made a suggestion, your turn will be complete. The Send Turn window will display, letting you know that the game has connected to the server and that your turn has been sent. Click the OK button to close the window. Afterward, you may view your notepad or the history, but you cannot make any more game moves. To close the game, click Exit Cluedo.


Using Netscape and AOLTOP é

  • Netscape Users If you are using Netscape to read your email, the first time you click on the attachment it will ask you if you want to Open or Save the attachment. We recommend that you open it.

  • AOL Users Some older versions of AOL do not allow for a true TCP\IP connection to the Internet. If you experience any problems sending turns with AOL try upgrading to AOL 4.0.

To open a turn file using AOL, you will first need to download it to your hard drive. The default directory for AOL downloads is: C:\America Online 4.0\download (for AOL 4.0), and C:\AOL3.0\download (for AOL 3.0). Then you will need to go to the appropriate folder and double-click on the downloaded file to open and play the turn.
