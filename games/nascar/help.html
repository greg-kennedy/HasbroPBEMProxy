Introduction

TOPs

The smell of burning rubber, the roar of the crowd. . . adrenaline courses through your veins as you bank hard into turn number four. You've been chasing the leader for what seems like an eternity, but you're close, real close. You slide into position and draft him, perspiration gluing your hands to your gloves; tension, your gloves to the wheel. One more lap to go. The checkered flag is unfurling. Victory lane awaits the winner. You take it high into turn number three, put the pedal down, and make your move.

Welcome to Hasbro's Em@il NASCAR, the game that puts you behind the wheel of your own racecar. Choose from a list of real NASCAR drivers like Jeff Gordon, Mark Martin, or seven-time Winston Cup Champion Dale Earnhardt as you try to maneuver your way to victory. Challenge friends and foes alike to some turn-based head-to-head competition.

But watch out! The track is littered with obstacles, and your buddies are closing in fast. Only your patience, poise, and ingenuity will earn you that coveted checkered flag and the sweet taste of victory in the Winner's Circle.

Set up and Installation:

TOPs

The ReadMe File
The Hasbro Interactive Em@il NASCAR CD-ROM game has a ReadMe file with which you can view both the License Agreement and updated information about the game. To view, double left-click on that file in the Em@il NASCAR directory found on your hard drive. You can also view the ReadMe file by first clicking on the START button on your Win 95/98 taskbar, then clicking on Programs, then on Hasbro Interactive, then on Em@il NASCAR, and finally on the ReadMe file item.

Em@il Games – A Brief Overview
Hasbro Interactive Em@il Games are played by sending turns back and forth via email to your opponent. To view your opponent's last move, simply open the attached 'TURN' file emailed to you by your opponent. This will automatically open up the proper Em@il Game and allow you to play the next move.

Starting a New Game

TOPs

You must have the Em@il NASCAR CD-ROM in your CD-ROM drive to begin a new game. Keep in mind though, your opponents do not have to have the CD-ROM disc in order to play. The player who starts a new game automatically becomes Player 1, and is the first to select from the list of drivers, sets options, and is first to play a turn for the upcoming game. This player is also the person who chooses who will participate in the game by adding email addresses subsequent to the completion of the initial turn.

Driver Selection Screen
Choose your driver from a list of NASCAR favorites, using the bright yellow frame to highlight the driver and car you wish to select. Left-click on the up and down arrows to advance through the list of drivers that are available. Default drivers included are: Dale Earnhardt, Jeff Gordon, Mark Martin, Rusty Wallace, Bobby Labonte, and Terry Labonte.
When your desired driver is highlighted, click on Next to proceed.

Increasing the Available Driver List
A complete set of NASCAR drivers can be downloaded for your enjoyment at the Em@il NASCAR home site at: http://email.games.com.*
*If a driver is selected that has not been downloaded by any of the other game participants, they will see that driver and his car as a gray default character for the duration of the game.

Alias and Strategy Selection
You will be prompted to enter your nickname for this game of Em@il NASCAR; your alias can be up to ten characters long. Then click 'OK' to go on to Strategy Selection. Here you will be presented with a choice of strategies. The Speed Bonus will give you +1 speed for each turn, but also a -2 control rating penalty. The Control Bonus will add a +2 control rating bonus. The Damage Bonus will reduce inflicted damage by 10%. Click in the circle next to your choice and then hit 'OK' to proceed.

Game Options*
Options that are currently selected are indicated by a black dot inside the white circle that is located to the left of the various choices. To change, left click on the white circle, making sure that a black dot appears next to the desired options.

*Only Player 1 has the ability to select options. This screen will not be available to the remaining players.

Minimal Hazards: Hazards include oil slicks only.

Normal Hazards: Both oil and debris increase the risk of damage to your vehicle.

Extreme Hazards: Oil, debris, and wreckage litter the track.

Skill Shot Mode: The skill shot is a test of the player's coordination by means of an advancing power meter bar, which determines speed and control ratings for the turn.

Die Roll Mode: Speed and control ratings are randomly generated per turn.

Laps: The number of laps can be manipulated by left-clicking on the up and down arrow buttons next to the lap number currently chosen. The number of laps can range from 1 to 10.

When finished choosing the Game Options, Player 1 advances to the next screen by clicking on OK.

Playing the Game - The Track Screen

TOPs

It's finally time to strap on your lucky helmet and get to the action. The Track Screen is where all of the fun and excitement of Em@il NASCAR actually takes place. Players must first determine their speed and control ratings by either skill shot or die roll mode (as determined by the Game Option Settings). Once a player's speed and control rating have been determined, he then has the option of using event cards and/or plotting his movement around the track.

Speed and Control Ratings

Speed Rating: The player's speed rating determines the amount of spaces a player's vehicle can move per turn. Speed ratings range from a minimum of 2 to a maximum of 10.

Control Rating: The player's control rating determines which hazards can be ignored and which will inflict damage to the player's vehicle if encountered that turn. Hazards will assume a gray appearance when rendered ineffective by a higher control rating. Control ratings range from a minimum of 2 to a maximum of 10.

Rate Determination Modes

Skill Shot Mode: The skill shot is a test of the player's coordination by means of advancing power meter bars, which determine speed and control ratings for the turn.

When the player is ready, he will left-click on the blinking red button to start the movement of the bar. The player then clicks a second time to stop the bar. The player's first attempt will determine the speed rating for the upcoming turn while the second attempt will determine the control rating.

When performing the speed skill shot, you are attempting to hold down the button until the advancing bar is as close to the end of the box as possible. The closer the red bar to the end of the box, the higher your speed rating. However, if you hold down too long and let the bar strike the end of the box, your scores will result in 4 Speed and 2 Control. If you do not overshoot on your speed skill shot, you will then perform the control skill shot. Here you are attempting to halt the blue control bar as close to the end of the red speed bar as possible. If you overshoot on this attempt, you will end up with a 2 Control rating, though this does not affect your Speed score.

Die Roll Mode: Speed and control ratings are randomly generated for the turn by left clicking on the blinking red button.

Moving Around the Track

TOPs

Once a player has determined speed and control ratings by either skill shot or die roll, he then has the ability to move his highlighted car around the track. Possible movements are characterized as an advancing yellow line that leads to a red button. To move to a certain space, click on the red button. The button will then be transformed into a solid circle, with the next set of movement options plotted as described above.

When a player has reached his maximum amount of movements that turn, no more plotting lines will appear.

Undoing Movements: A player can take back movements at any time by left clicking on 'UNDO', located in the bottom right corner of the Track screen.

Damage Meter
A player's vehicle can take damage in one of two ways:

  1. By running into a hazard that has not been overcome by a sufficient control rating.
  2. By having a Hazard Card played on his vehicle by an opponent.

Accumulated damage is recorded by the Damage Meter, located to the left of the space allotted for the player's event cards.

Reading the Damage Meter: To check the damage meter, position the mouse pointer over it. The current damage readout will appear in the bottom left corner of the Track Screen, under the word 'UNDO'.

Damage Effects: Damage adversely affects vehicle performance, reducing speed and control ratings accordingly. A maximum damage rating of 90% will automatically set speed and control ratings to a score of 2 until the player has a chance to visit the pits for repairs.

Hazards
Hazards occupy a space on the racing track and can inflict damage to any car that enters that space.

Rendering Hazards Ineffective: Only a high control rating will allow a vehicle to move into a space occupied by a hazard without sustaining damage. Hazards that have been rendered ineffective for the turn by a high control rating become grayed out.

Oil: Oil on the track is a fairly common occurrence, requiring only a moderate control score of five or better to overcome. Oil inflicts 10% damage to a vehicle that does not have an adequate control score.

Debris: Debris is a moderate hazard that includes random pieces of flotsam and jetsam that accumulate on the track. Debris inflicts 20% damage to a vehicle that does not have a control score of eight or better.

Wreckage: Wreckage is a major hazard that has the ability to inflict extensive damage to a vehicle that enters the space it occupies. Only with a control score of a perfect ten can a player drive through wreckage without mishap. Wreckage inflicts 30% damage to a vehicle that fails to avoid it.

Event Cards
Each player starts the game with four event cards. Players can implement event cards by dragging and releasing the desired card either over their own vehicle or the vehicle of an opponent.

Hazard Cards:

    1. Oil: Allows player to place an oil hazard on an opponent's vehicle. This causes the opponent to sustain 10% damage to their vehicle and places an oil hazard on the space where the card was played.
    2. Debris: Allows player to place a debris hazard on an opponent's vehicle. This causes the opponent to sustain 20% damage to their vehicle and places a debris hazard on the space where the card was played.

Disaster Cards:

    1. Engine Fire: Causes opponent's engine to burst into flames, giving him a maximum speed rating of 2 until he visits the pits.
    2. Lost a Wheel: Causes opponent's vehicle to lose a wheel, giving him a maximum control rating of 2 and a maximum speed rating of 6 until he visits the pits.
    3. Worn Tires: Causes opponent's tires to become severely worn, reducing his speed and control ratings by 5 points until he visits the pits.

Windfall Cards:

    1. Drafting: Find a car in front and become its personal bumper sticker. Grants an increase of 5 points to the speed rating for the turn.
    2. In the Zone: Driving focus is unmatched, increasing the control rating by 5 points for the turn.
    3. Lead Foot: Put the pedal to the metal. This card increases the speed rating by 5 points, but the control rating suffers, resulting in a decrease of 5 points for the turn.
    4. Sticker Tires: Grants you unparalleled traction, resulting in an increase of 5 points to both the speed and control ratings for the turn.
    5. Super Reflexes: Your reflexes are extraordinary, giving you a control rating of 10 for that turn.

Reading the Event Cards
To check the effect of any given Event Card, position the mouse pointer over it. The Event Card's effect will appear on the bottom of the Track Screen.

The Pits
Whenever his car is on the adjacent space to the pits entrance, a player has the option of moving into the pits for repairs. By ending his turn in the pits, done so by hitting 'Send' in the bottom right corner, the vehicle's damage meter is reset to zero and four new Event Cards are drawn for the player. The next turn he will emerge with a fully repaired vehicle, fresh Event Cards, and ready to catch up with the pack.

Lap Counter
The Lap Counter is represented by two numbers that are separated by a slash (/), located in the upper left corner of the Track Screen. The number to the left of the slash shows the lap the player is currently on. The number to the right of the slash displays the amount of laps that the current game has been set for.

Sending Messages

TOPs

Part of the fun of multi-player games is the ability to send and receive personalized messages between opponents. Em@il NASCAR has made sure to include this functionality, giving players the ability to express their opinions, be it on the road to victory, or overwhelmed by the agony of defeat.*

*Hasbro Interactive does not monitor, control, endorse, or accept responsibility for the content of text messages through the use of this product. Use of the optional Text Message feature considered to be conducted at your own risk. Users are strongly encouraged not to give out personal information through text message transmissions.

Sending Messages*

  1. To compose a message, a player must left-click on the 'Send Message' icon located on the upper right side of the Track Screen. The icon is represented by a hand holding a pencil.
  2. A window will open that lists the names of all players in the game. Highlighting a name allows the player to enter the text message he wishes to forward to that opponent. There is also an "All" option for writing a message to all players.
  3. When finished composing a message, left-click on 'OK'. It will be sent to the desired opponent(s) to be received at the start of their next turn.

*Since Player 1 uses his initial turn for game setup and opponent indication, this functionality is unavailable to all players until the second turn.

Receiving Messages

  1. When a player has received a message from an opponent, it is signified by the blinking of the 'View Message' icon located on the upper right side of the Track Screen. The 'View Message' icon is represented by an envelope.
  2. To view the message, a player must left-click on the 'View Message' icon. Upon doing so, a window will open that contains the message.
  3. When finished reading the message, left-click 'OK'.

Sending Your Turn

TOPs

When satisfied with movement, Event Card usage, and message composition, a player submits a turn by clicking on 'Go', located in the bottom right corner of the Track Screen. Upon doing so, his vehicle will advance along the path chosen when he plotted his movements. Also, the Write Messages box will appear in case the player wishes to compose a final message to a competitor. The 'Go' button will be replaced by the 'Send' button. Clicking this will forward the player's move for the round, as well as the moves of the players that proceeded him.

Selecting Opponents (Player 1 only)

TOPs

Upon the completion of Player 1's initial turn, a window will appear that allows that player to select up to five opponents to participate in the game. The player can do so by either manually entering the email addresses of desired participants on one of the lines provided, or by using the pull-down menu to the right of one of the gray address lines. This will institute the Address Book functionality that accompanies the game.

Manual Entry: To manually enter an email address onto one of the five gray address lines provided, just left-click upon it. A flashing cursor will appear, enabling the player to type in the address desired. Upon doing so, the address will be added to the Address Book list, and it will be later available for easy access through that functionality.

The Address Book: Any email address that has been entered to signify a participant in a game of Em@il NASCAR will be stored in the address book. Access the compiled list by left-clicking on the down arrow that is located at the right of one of the gray address lines. Choose a potential participant by double left-clicking on the email address desired. The list access window will close, and that person's email address will appear in the chosen line.

Deleting Addresses from the Address Book: To include a person as a participant in any given game, check the white box to the left of their email address by left-clicking upon it. A checkmark should appear within the white box next to the email addresses of all desired participants.

Once satisfied with the choices of participants for the upcoming game, left-click on 'OK'.*

Unused Vehicles
Six vehicles are always involved in any given race. Any vehicles not used by email opponents will be controlled automatically, adding those vehicles to the obstacles that a player must overcome to achieve victory.

Exiting the Game

TOPs

Once a player's turn is completed and has been sent to the next player by clicking on 'GO', left-click on 'Send' in the bottom right corner of the Track Screen to exit the game.

Receiving and Playing Turns

TOPs

In Em@il NASCAR, turns are forwarded from player to player via email. When a player's turn is ready to be played, he will receive an email that is named 'NASCAR Move'. Inside that email is a file attachment that is named 'Turn'. If the player's email program allows him to open files directly, he just needs to double left-click on the attachment to open it and play his turn. If the email program does not allow for opening files directly, the 'Turn' file must first be saved to the computer (the desktop is always an easy location) and double left-clicked to open. Either way, the player will advance to the Standings Screen.

Standing Screen
The Standings Screen displays the current 1st, 2nd, and 3rd place drivers for the race in progress, ranked accordingly.
When finished with this screen, the player advances to the Track Screen, and their next turn, by left-clicking on 'OK'.

Winning the Game

TOPs

The winner is the first person to cross the finish line, thus completing the amount of laps chosen during the Game Options Setup for the current game. The second and third place drivers are the players who were next closest to completing the race when the game ended.

The Winner's Circle
Once the top three finishers has been decided, one final email will be sent to all the players who participated in the game, depicting the 1st place finisher in the winner's circle, with the 2nd and 3rd place drivers underneath.
